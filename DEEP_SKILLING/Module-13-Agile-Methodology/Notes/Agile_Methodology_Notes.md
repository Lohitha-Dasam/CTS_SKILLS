# Agile Methodology Notes

## 1. What is Agile?

Agile is a software development methodology that focuses on delivering software incrementally through collaboration, customer feedback, and continuous improvement.

### Goals of Agile

- Deliver working software frequently
- Adapt to changing requirements
- Improve customer satisfaction
- Encourage teamwork and collaboration
- Increase product quality

---

## 2. Agile Manifesto

### Four Core Values

1. Individuals and interactions over processes and tools.
2. Working software over comprehensive documentation.
3. Customer collaboration over contract negotiation.
4. Responding to change over following a plan.

---

### Twelve Agile Principles

- Customer satisfaction through early and continuous delivery.
- Welcome changing requirements.
- Deliver working software frequently.
- Business and developers work together daily.
- Build projects around motivated individuals.
- Face-to-face communication is most effective.
- Working software is the primary measure of progress.
- Maintain a sustainable development pace.
- Continuous attention to technical excellence.
- Simplicity is essential.
- Self-organizing teams produce the best designs.
- Regularly reflect and improve.

---

## 3. Agile vs Waterfall

| Agile | Waterfall |
|--------|-----------|
| Iterative development | Sequential development |
| Flexible | Rigid |
| Continuous feedback | Feedback at the end |
| Customer involvement throughout | Limited customer involvement |
| Frequent releases | Single final release |

---

## 4. Scrum Framework

Scrum is the most widely used Agile framework.

### Scrum Roles

### Product Owner

- Defines product vision
- Maintains Product Backlog
- Prioritizes requirements

### Scrum Master

- Facilitates Scrum practices
- Removes obstacles
- Helps the team follow Agile principles

### Development Team

- Designs
- Develops
- Tests
- Delivers product increments

---

## 5. Scrum Ceremonies

### Sprint Planning

Plan the work for the upcoming sprint.

### Daily Scrum

15-minute daily meeting to discuss:

- What was completed yesterday?
- What will be done today?
- Any blockers?

### Sprint Review

Demonstrate completed work to stakeholders.

### Sprint Retrospective

Discuss improvements for the next sprint.

---

## 6. Scrum Artifacts

### Product Backlog

Complete list of project requirements.

### Sprint Backlog

Tasks selected for the current sprint.

### Increment

Working product delivered at the end of a sprint.

### Definition of Done

Criteria that determine when work is considered complete.

---

## 7. Agile Estimation

### Story Points

Measure the complexity of a user story.

Example:

- 1 → Very Easy
- 2 → Easy
- 3 → Medium
- 5 → Complex
- 8 → Very Complex
- 13 → Highly Complex

---

### Planning Poker

Planning Poker is an estimation technique where team members estimate story points using numbered cards.

Benefits:

- Better estimation
- Team collaboration
- Avoids bias

---

## 8. Sprint Planning

Sprint Planning determines:

- Sprint Goal
- Tasks to complete
- Estimated effort
- Sprint duration

---

## 9. Velocity

Velocity measures how many story points a team completes in one sprint.

Example:

Sprint 1 → 25 Story Points

Sprint 2 → 30 Story Points

Average Velocity = 27.5 Story Points

---

## 10. Burndown Chart

A Burndown Chart shows:

- Remaining work
- Sprint progress
- Expected completion

It helps track whether the sprint is on schedule.

---

## 11. User Stories

A User Story describes a feature from the user's perspective.

Format:

As a <user>

I want <goal>

So that <benefit>

Example:

As a customer,

I want to reset my password,

So that I can regain access to my account.

---

## 12. INVEST Principle

Good user stories follow INVEST.

- Independent
- Negotiable
- Valuable
- Estimable
- Small
- Testable

---

## 13. Acceptance Criteria

Acceptance Criteria define when a user story is complete.

### Given-When-Then Format

Given the user is on the login page

When valid credentials are entered

Then the user should be redirected to the dashboard.

---

## 14. Agile Team Collaboration

An Agile team works together by:

- Continuous communication
- Daily Scrum meetings
- Frequent feedback
- Shared responsibility
- Continuous improvement

---

## Summary

Agile is an iterative software development methodology focused on collaboration, adaptability, and delivering customer value. Scrum, User Stories, Sprint Planning, Story Points, Planning Poker, and Acceptance Criteria help Agile teams deliver high-quality software efficiently.
